# Purpose

Miniprep the liquid cultures of the recA or CpxP plasmid-containing *E. coli*, and send the plasmids for sequencing.

# Materials & Methods

NEB Monarch® Plasmid Miniprep Kit
NanoDrop 1000 Spectrophotometer
Plasmidsaurus Full Plasmid Sequencing

**Miniprep is based on the **[**manufacturer’s instructions**](https://www.neb.com/en-ca/protocols/monarch-plasmid-dna-miniprep-kit-protocol-t1010)**:**

1. 200 uL RNAse buffer
2. 200 uL Lysis buffer
   1. invert the tube 5-6 times
   2. Wait 1 minute
3. 400 uL Neutralization buffer
   1. invert the tube 5-6 times
   2. Wait 2 minutes
4. Spin at 10000 x g for 5 minutes
5. 800 uL of supernatant into the column in a collection tube
6. Spin at 1000 x g for 5 minutes
   1. Pour the flow-through into the column again and re-spin at 1000 x g for 5 minutes
   2. Discard flow-through
7. 200 uL of wash 1
8. Spin at 10000 x g for 1 minute
9. 400 uL of wash 2
10. Spin at 10000 x g for 1 minute
11. Discard the collection tube and place the miniprep column in a clean microcentrifuge tube
12. 45 uL of pre-warmed H2O
    1. incubate at room temperature for 3-4 minutes
    2. Spin at 10000 x g for 1 minute

**Nanodrop-1000 Spectrophotometer**

1. Measure 2 uL of eluted plasmid using the Nanodrop-1000
2. Record DNA concentration (ng/uL) and 260/280 ratio

Note: the ideal 260/280 ratio is 1.80.

**Plasmidsaurus full-plasmid sequencing is based on Plasmidsaurus instructions:**

1. Add 500 ng of DNA into a PCR tube
2. Top up to 15 uL total volume with H2O
3. Request sequencing using the Plasmidsaurus website and drop off the sample at a designated pick-up location (ie, SBME)

# Results

Miniprep yielded high concentrations of DNA for both recA and CpxP plasmids.

- RecA plasmid: **99.8 ng/uL** and\*\* 260/280: 1.95\*\*

![Example Nanodrop result for recA plasmid. Notice the concentration, and 260/280 peak and ratio.](https://TODO.com)

- CpxP plasmid: **94.1 ng/uL** and **260/280: 1.96**

Sequencing results yielded successfully cloned plasmids.

- recA plasmid: 100% matched to template DNA

![Sequencing results for recA plasmid show 100% match to the template DNA.](https://TODO.com)

- CpxP plasmid: contained 3 point mutations
  - First mutation → behind ORI sequence → does not matter for our application
  - Second mutation → silent mutation on Cm selection gene → no effect
  - Third mutation → missense mutation on Cm selection gene (Glycine → Valine)
    - The colonies are still able to grow on antibiotic-containing plates
    - The CpxP promoter and sfGFP gene are unaffected → should be fully functional!

![Sequencing results for CpxP plasmid show 3 point mutations all outside the important region of the CpxP promoter and sfGFP gene.](https://TODO.com)

# Summary

The cloned recA and CpxP plasmids are successful based on sequencing results and can be transformed into our biosensor strains (*E. coli K12 derivatives*) for testing validation.
