# Purpose

Transform the cloned plasmids (recA and CpxP) into high efficiency competent DH5a and plate.

# Materials & Methods

Chloramphenicol LB agar plate (17 mg/L Cm concentration)
NEB DH5a high-efficiency chemically competent *E. coli*
Cloned plasmids (recA and CpxP) from [Notebook Entry 1](/wet-lab/lab-notebook-2026/notebook-entry-1)
NEB SOC Outgrowth Medium
42°C water bath

1. Mix 9 µL of chemically competent DH5a bacteria with 1 µL of cloned plasmid (recA or CpxP)
   1. pipette up and down to mix
2. Incubate on ice for 30 minutes
3. Heat shock at 42°C for 30 seconds
4. Incubate on ice for 5 minutes
5. Add 100 µL\*\* \*\*of SOC outgrowth medium and incubate at 37°C for 1 hour
6. Plate 50 uL on each plate and incubate at 37°C overnight

# Results

Overnight incubation resulted in single colonies of CpxP- and recA-transformed plasmids.

The transformation and plating of RecA and CpxP plasmids were successful as single colonies were obtained.
